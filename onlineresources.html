1. Textbook

Practical tips: Textbooks are often the gateway to a subject, and so is economics. But active engagement is key to truly learning. Instead of reading passively or simply highlighting, focus on capturing key points by writing them down. This method not only helps you memorize important concepts but also provides you with a personalized study guide for revising later. Writing engages your brain more effectively than skimming or highlighting, especially when faced with dense material. So, when you are reading a textbook after downloading it, remember: make each note count for better focus and retention!

2. Online Notes

This is one of the most comprehensive and complete sets of class notes for IB Economics students, written by Jason Welker, a renowned YouTuber known for teaching economics. These notes contain various pieces of knowledge, graphs, and formulas. You don’t need to copy everything down—this resource can serve as an example to help you understand the outline of economics and discover effective ways to memorize key points.

3. Study guides

The study guide includes seven sections to help you pass the exam. It contains figures and guidance for tackling real-exam problems, understanding the definition of economic terms, and writing your economic essay.

4. Khan Academy

Khan Academy isn’t just a tutorial website—it’s your gateway to mastering microeconomics. Imagine diving into a series of engaging learning videos that don’t just teach concepts but bring economics to life. Each video breaks down complex ideas, making them easy to grasp, while interactive questions push you to test your understanding and cement what you’ve learned. It's like having a personal economics coach guiding you every step of the way. Khan Academy doesn’t just deliver knowledge; it empowers you to truly own it, making economics a subject you can confidently tackle, understand, and even enjoy.
